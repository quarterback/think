Think Studio

Speculative design / solo practice

Artist bio

Blunt objects, printed scores, and room-sized systems for rehearsing damaged futures.

The practice begins with things you can name: dust on sensors, taped seams, cheap speakers, paper instructions, borrowed chairs, and light leaking under a door. These materials become tests for how people live with broken tools, unstable weather, and archives that remember too much or not enough.

Projects

Selected work

  • Weather for Unfinished Rooms

    Air sensors, fog, bare bulbs, and a subwoofer turn a sealed room into a weather report visitors can stand inside.

  • Manual for Soft Machines

    Stapled pages, annotated diagrams, and repair notes describe how to keep obsolete devices useful for one more day.

  • Archive That Dreams Back

    A box of cards, receipts, screenshots, and fabric samples stages an archive that misremembers its own sources.

  • Domestic Futures Index

    A deck of heavy cards asks players to rebuild kitchens, bedrooms, and bathrooms around scarcity and mutual repair.

  • Signals for Low Carbon Grief

    A pocket speaker and folded listening score mark the small comforts people may need to give up, slowly and together.
